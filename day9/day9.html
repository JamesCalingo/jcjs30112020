So...you're probably wondering why today's "app" looks just like one of my blog posts.

That's because, like days 4 and 7, there's no app today: it's just pure JS stuff. Unlike those days, however, we're not doing anything with array methods; instead, we're going to discuss

When I did this, I was under the understanding that computers “alphabetize” numbers based on their first digit - no matter how long the number is. Say you had a bunch of files numbered 1-19. If you sorted these files by name, you might be surprised when it shows up as

  • 1
  • 10
  • 11
  • 12
  • and so on

instead of

  • 1
  • 2
  • 3
  • 4
  • and so on

By putting a 0 in front of the digit for single digit numbers, we can essentially “trick” the computer into thinking they’re first in order, which they’re supposed to be.

However, a little poking around showed that this wasn’t the case - the computer can count properly without taking the tens digit into account. This may be due to the fact that I have the files as “dayxx” as opposed to starting with the numbers, but I’m honestly not sure.

Also, the reason I started at 0 is because that’s where a lot of programming languages start counting for those who weren’t aware (you get used to the after a while). It also made sense to me because I wanted it to represent the sort of "pre-project" phase - not doing any actual JS30 work, just getting the environment for it ready.

P.S.: There is a reason I kept 2 as 02 though. It’s a reference to one of my favorite television shows from my childhood - if you get it, then you’re awesome, but if you don’t, then I’m not explaining it to you. Sorry.
